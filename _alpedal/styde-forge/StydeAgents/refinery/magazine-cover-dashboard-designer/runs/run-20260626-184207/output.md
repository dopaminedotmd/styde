Mockup 1 — The Lead Story Layout
Cover Headline: REVENUE BREAKS $2.4B
Subhead: Q3 Growth Spikes 18% — Analysts Caught Flat
| Revenue $2.4B +18% | Net Income $487M +22% |
| COGS $1.1B +8% | Gross Margin 54% +3pp |
| Operating Cash $312M +41% | CAPEX $89M -12% |
| ARR $1.8B +31% | Churn 4.2% -0.8pp |
Rationale: Revenue dominates the left-center because it is the single most tracked top-line metric for quarterly earnings, establishing the narrative before any supporting data is read. Net Income sits adjacent because profit story is the second question every executive asks after top-line. COGS pairs with Revenue left-flush to anchor the cost conversation before moving right to the resulting margin story. Operating Cash surges to right-center because cash flow trajectory is the hidden signal that reveals whether growth is healthy or burning runway. CAPEX shrinking is the editorial counter-narrative — capital discipline in a growth story. ARR anchors bottom-left as the forward-looking subscription signal, deliberately separated from the transactional revenue number above. Churn closes the grid as the final emotional note because low churn justifies the valuation story the ARR implies.
