Holographic Dashboard — 10 Unique Mockups
--- MOCKUP 1: Quantum Terminal (holo-quantum-terminal.html)
this is a fullscreen terminal with glassmorphism panels and a live particle grid background. top-left: system status bar (fps, latency, uptime) in cyan monospace. center: a large 3D wireframe globe rendered in CSS-only with rotating neon rings. right rail: a scrolling log feed with translucent entries, each pulse-glowing on new data. bottom: a radial command input — circular, with concentric rings that brighten on hover. color stops: #00f0ff (primary cyan), #ff00aa (accent magenta), #0a0a1a (deep space).
--- MOCKUP 2: Aurora Dashboard (holo-aurora.html)
headerless, borderless. three equal vertical columns on a dark gradient that shifts slow from #0b0e2a to #1a0a2e. left column: metric cards with floating digits that have a blur-shadow under them as if floating above glass. middle column: a hexagonal grid chart — each hexagon is a data node, connected by thin glowing lines that animate with a wave pattern. right column: a vertical timeline/progress bar made of stacked translucent pills. the background aurora is a CSS gradient animation shifting cyan→purple→pink over 12 seconds.
--- MOCKUP 3: Pulse Monitor (holo-pulse-monitor.html)
medical-scifi inspired. single-panel centered layout. a large circular gauge made of repeating conic gradients with a floating needle. around it: four quadrant cards (top-left: heart-rate line chart in green neon, top-right: oxygen saturation ring, bottom-left: temperature bar, bottom-right: activity burst graph). every card has a slight tilt and a shadow that shifts as if tracking a light source. background: animated dots on a grid that fade in and out like breathing. colors: #00ff88 (primary), #0055ff (secondary), #0a0015 (bg).
--- MOCKUP 4: Hologlobe Control (holo-globe-control.html)
a command-center dashboard. top ribbon: 6 icon buttons with only a thin underline indicator for active state — no backgrounds. main area: a large abstract globe made of layered rotating rings (not a real map, just orbital paths). left of globe: a vertical slider with gradient fill from red to green, the thumb is a glowing dot. right: three stacked data chips — small translucent rectangles with two lines of data and a sparkline. bottom: a waveform visualization that responds to "audio" (we can fake it with a CSS animation on multiple bars of varying heights). dark bg with subtle film-grain overlay.
--- MOCKUP 5: Vector Dash (holo-vector.html)
angular, sharp, isometric-inspired. the entire layout is tilted slightly (transform: rotateX(2deg)). left panel: isometric cube widgets — each cube face shows a different metric. rotating on hover. center: a line chart that looks like a mountain range, drawn with thin neon strokes, with a gradient fill from the line to bottom that has 30% opacity. right panel: a grid of micro-cards (8 cards, 2x4) each showing a single number and a tiny icon, arranged with varying gap sizes. the whole layout uses a neon grid background. colors: yellow-neon #ffdd00, cyan #00eeff, deep navy #050510.
--- MOCKUP 6: Nebula Feed (holo-nebula.html)
a social/analytics hybrid feed. the background is a CSS nebula — multiple large radial gradients overlapping and slowly drifting. on it: a stack of feed cards, each with a small avatar ring (with glow), a title line, and a horizontal bar chart (for engagement). the cards have frosted-glass backsplash and slide in from the right on load. top bar: a search pill with inner glow. right sidebar: trending topics as floating tags that orbit slowly. colors: purple #aa44ff, hot pink #ff2266, dark indigo #0a0414.
--- MOCKUP 7: Orbit Panel (holo-orbit.html)
radial interface. everything orbits around a center point. a central circle with the main KPI number, surrounded by 6 smaller circles connected by thin lines (like an atom). each orbiting circle is a data point — when hovered, it expands into a small info panel with a mini-chart. the orbiting items move in elliptical paths (CSS keyframes with different durations). background: deep space with subtle twinkling stars (randomly placed small dots with varying opacity animation). primary: #00aaff, secondary: #ff66aa.
--- MOCKUP 8: Glass Tower (holo-glass-tower.html)
vertical skyscraper metaphor. a tall narrow panel on the left showing a vertical building-like structure where each "floor" is a metric bar — the bar fills like a progress indicator, glowing brighter as it fills (from dim blue at 10% to bright cyan at 100%). main area: a large grid of translucent panes (3 columns x 3 rows), each pane contains a different widget type (pie, bar, donut, number, list). the panes have 1px borders with glow and when hovered the glow intensifies. right sidebar: a compact timeline with dots connected by a vertical line. background: light rays emanating from the center using conic gradients.
--- MOCKUP 9: Synthwave Terminal (holo-synthwave.html)
80s-meets-future. a dark background with a horizontal sun line gradient at the top (pink to purple). the main content is in a large terminal-style window with scanlines overlay. inside: monospace data but with neon accents — cyan prompts, magenta values, yellow warnings. data is displayed as a table with no grid lines, just light dots at each data point intersection. bottom: a command bar where the cursor blinks. background: a retro grid that extends into the distance (perspective transform). colors: #ff2277, #ffaa00, #00ffcc, #1a0030.
--- MOCKUP 10: Zero-Frame (holo-zero-frame.html)
no borders, no cards, no panels — information floats directly on the background. text with drop-shadow and glow directly on a dark canvas. a large central time display (digital clock style) with each digit having a subtle flip animation. scattered around it: small groups of data that have only a thin underline (not a card), floating at different z-levels using drop-shadow size. a thin horizontal line runs across the middle third with small dots indicating key events. background: smooth gradient from #03010e to #0d0420 with occasional "data packets" that zip across (small bright dots with trailing glow, animated in random paths).
